Bias

Welcome to BIAS ONLINE: a digital exhibition space by Science Gallery at Trinity College Dublin showcasing digital artworks exploring data equity, privacy, surveillance culture, facial recognition, class and artificial intelligence. Our online exhibition takes four of the key pieces from the exhibition and asks questions about class, race, what we deem to be "normal" and how all that is translated by machines, measured and written into data. Scroll down to explore works by Johann Diedrick and Noah Levenson, and new commissions from Libby Heaney and Mushon Zer-Aviv.

This virtual exhibition is best experienced on desktop with your browser window in full screen mode. For Mushon Zer-Aviv's artwork NORMALIZI.NG, you will need to use your phone to interact with the exhibit.

From cognitive function to machine learning, bias is a shortcut, for our brain or for data. This virtual exhibition is part of Science Gallery Dublin’s year-long BIAS season, interrogating how bias moves from human to machine and how persuasion, preference, motivation and misinformation contribute to our individual, societal and digital biases.

Science Gallery Dublin is part of the Global Science Gallery Network pioneered by Trinity College Dublin.

BIAS ONLINE was developed with the support of the European ARTificial Intelligence Lab, co-funded by the Creative Europe Programme of the European Union.

BIAS ONLINE was created with the support of The Department of Tourism, Culture, Arts, Gaeltacht, Sport and Media.
